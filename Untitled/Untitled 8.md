### 1. Overview of the University of Calgary
The University of Calgary is a public research university located in Calgary, Alberta, Canada, founded in 1966. It serves over 35,000 students (including undergraduates, graduates, and postdocs) across more than 250 programs in fields like engineering, business, health sciences, arts, and sciences. It's known for its emphasis on innovation, entrepreneurship, and transdisciplinary research—meaning programs that blend multiple disciplines, such as combining engineering with environmental studies or health with data science.

Key stats (based on recent university data and rankings):
- **Rankings**: QS World University Rankings places it at #211 globally as of 2025, with an overall score of 4.3/5 stars. It's often ranked in the top 10 in Canada for research impact and employability.
- **Campus**: Spans 213 hectares with modern facilities, including the Taylor Family Digital Library, Olympic Oval (from the 1988 Winter Olympics), and research centers like the Hotchkiss Brain Institute. It's in a suburban area with easy access to downtown Calgary and the Rocky Mountains.
- **Tuition and Costs**: Domestic undergraduates pay around CAD 6,000–8,000 per year; international students pay CAD 25,000–30,000. Living costs in Calgary are moderate compared to Toronto or Vancouver.
- **Diversity**: About 25% of students are international, with strong equity, diversity, and inclusion (EDI) initiatives, as highlighted in university reports.

UCalgary positions itself as a "next-generation" university, focusing on community engagement and real-world problem-solving. However, it has faced challenges like funding cuts, enrollment caps on international students (announced in 2024), and occasional controversies over governance.
